About

I’m an Animation Supervisor with a background in film and creative production. I enjoy shaping ideas into engaging, story-driven work through collaboration and attention to detail. I’ve been lucky to work on a wide range of projects with talented teams, creating work that resonates with audiences. I’m motivated by thoughtful storytelling, strong collaboration, and the opportunity to take on new creative challenges.

Kraven (2024) – Animation Supervisor
One of the most memorable sequences we had done was the intense battle between Rhino and Kraven. The buffalo stampede was another standout, along with the grizzly bear, lion, and wolves, all of which added a unique layer of realism and intensity to the film. Particular attention was given to conveying the sheer weight and physicality of the Rhino, which was critical in grounding the action and making the confrontation feel truly impactful.

Atlas (2024) – Animation Supervisor
We brought a grounded realism to the ARC suits by blending human reference with the mechanical weight and structure of their design. Our team animated several key sequences, including the attack on the ICN spacecraft, Atlas’s ejection and atmospheric descent in the ARC suit, and her initial escape from the planet prior to confronting Harlan.

Dungeons & Dragons: Honor Among Thieves (2023) – Animation Supervisor
Working on the diverse range of D&D creatures in this film was an absolute blast. I'm especially proud of the sequences we animated for Themberchaud, the massive red dragon whose imposing presence was balanced with a touch of comedic charm. Our team also led several standout moments, including Doric’s dynamic morphing castle escape sequence, the comedic Jarnathan tower escape, the portal carriage infiltration, the graveyard corpses, the Rakor black dragon flashback, and the intellect devourer scene.

Sonic the Hedgehog 2 (2022) – Animation Supervisor
Our team brought excitement to some of the film’s most dynamic moments, including the high-speed snowboarding escape from Robotnik’s drones and Sonic’s heroic run across the ocean, navigating massive waves. I’m especially proud of our work on the final climactic battle where Sonic, Knuckles, and Tails are surrounded by drones and charge toward Robotnik in a fast-paced, pivotal sequence.

Godzilla vs. Kong (2021) – Animation Supervisor
I'm extremely proud of the animation we contributed to this film, it was a creatively rewarding experience, especially in helping develop new sequence ideas with the director. We animated the epic final battle in Hong Kong, as well as the more emotional moments with Jia. I’ve never taken more bumps and bruises from shooting reference than I did on GvK.

Underwater (2020) – Animation Supervisor
This was one of the most technically demanding yet rewarding projects I’ve worked on, with creature rigs that required a high level of artistry and control. One creature initially featured thirteen tentacles before evolving into a more bipedal design. We also worked on the massive Behemoth/Cthulhu and its spawn, sequences that combined scale, mood, and impact.

X-Men: Dark Phoenix (2019) – Animation Supervisor
Our team animated the film’s climactic train skirmish sequence, delivering a high-energy finale that brought the action to a dramatic peak. We handled a large number of digi-double shots throughout the film, aiming to achieve dynamic poses and movement inspired by classic X-Men comics. We also had a specialized team focused on enhanced rotoanim for Jean’s facial energy tracking, ensuring a seamless integration of performance and visual effects.

Jumanji: Welcome to the Jungle (2017) – Lead Animator
I had the opportunity to lead on the jaguar, elephant, and snake sequences, each bringing a sense of danger and intensity to the film. The sequences were a frequent source of laughter for the team, it was just a great comedic film to be a part of.

Wonder Woman (2017) – Lead Animator
I served as Lead on several of the film’s standout sequences, including the beach skirmish, the war trenches battle, and the dramatic liberation of Veld, each presenting unique creative and technical challenges. The production was an exciting, collaborative experience.

Suicide Squad (2016) – Lead Animator
Our team brought several supernatural elements to life, including the faceless Eyes of the Adversary with their unsettling black, fluid-like eyes. We also worked on the Adversary’s head-shattering shots, a technically complex effect requiring precise rigging. Additionally, we animated the Enchantress and her magical abilities, blending character performance with her otherworldly presence.

The Legend of Tarzan (2016) – Lead Animator
I was a Lead on the lion and wildebeest stampede sequences, which involved complex interaction work, particularly the lion shots featuring close contact with Tarzan. We animated numerous hero wildebeest, and for larger-scale moments, developed crowd elements using cycles and clips to maintain visual consistency and energy.

Goosebumps (2015) – Lead Animator
I was a Lead on several of the film’s standout creatures and sequences, including the giant praying mantis, ventriloquist dummy Slappy, the evil poodle, the mischievous garden gnomes, the high school siege, and the final battle. It was a fun, fast-paced project and a memorable first experience as a Lead, both creatively fulfilling and an absolute blast to work on.

X-Men: Days of Future Past (2014) – Senior Animator
I focused primarily on the future Sentinels, but also animated a variety of digi-doubles, including Iceman. It was an incredible experience to contribute to some of the film’s most dynamic sequences, especially the final future battle where the combination of Sentinels and Blink’s portals made for a visually striking and uniquely challenging animation sequence.

The Smurfs 2 (2013) – Senior Animator
Another really fun show to work on, I had the opportunity to animate many of the Smurfs, including the newly introduced Naughty Smurfs. It was a playful and character driven project that offered plenty of variety and charm in the performances.

R.I.P.D. (2013) – Senior Animator
A darkly funny film with a unique tone, this project gave me the chance to focus primarily on the character Nawicki in a memorable scene where he reveals the gold he’s been hiding. It was a great blend of character work and stylized storytelling.

Escape from Planet Earth (2012) – Senior Animator
This fully animated project was a great opportunity to really push character performance, adding plenty of appeal and exaggeration to the animation. Despite going through numerous script rewrites, the film ultimately came together as a fun and lively piece that was a joy to work on.

The Amazing Spider-Man (2012) – Senior Animator
This was a fantastic project to animate on, with a great mix of action and character-driven moments. I primarily focused on Spider-Man and the Lizard, contributing to key sequences including their intense battle in the school and the large-scale confrontation in New York City as the Lizard unleashes his mutating green gas across the city.

The Smurfs (2011) – Senior Animator
I had the chance to animate a variety of Smurf characters, making it a particularly fun and nostalgic project. Having grown up watching the Peyo-inspired cartoon series, it was a real joy to help bring these beloved little characters to life with personality and charm.

Gnomeo & Juliet (2011) – Senior Animator
A fun and charming film with a fantastic soundtrack, this project was a joy to be part of. I was lucky to animate many of the film’s characters, but especially enjoyed working on the mushroom character "Shroom," who posed a unique and rewarding challenge of expressing a full range of emotions without a face!

The Chronicles of Narnia: The Voyage of the Dawn Treader (2010) – Senior Animator
This was a magical and truly enjoyable project to be part of. I had the opportunity to animate Reepicheep the brave and noble talking mouse, as well as scenes featuring Dragon Eustace, both of which brought a mix of charm, fantasy, and character-driven storytelling to the film.

District 9 (2009) – Animator
An incredible film to be part of, District 9 was both critically acclaimed and nominated for Best Motion Picture and Best Visual Effects at the 2010 Academy Awards. I focused on animating the character of Christopher Johnson, the main alien (or "prawn"), a role that offered a rich blend of performance, subtle emotion, and visual storytelling.

The Wild (2006) – Animator
I started my film career in 2006 as an Animator on The Wild which was the largest animated film ever produced in Canada up to that point. I animated many of the quadruped characters in the film but mostly focused on the squirrel named "Benny" voiced by Jim Belushi. It was great having Jim's unique video reference to enhance some of Benny's acting and performance.
